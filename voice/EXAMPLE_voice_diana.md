## Tonal Range

| Situation | Tone |
|---|---|
| First reply to a new prospect | Warm, specific, action-oriented |
| Status update to active client | Concise, factual, named next step |
| Sensitive (competing offer, inspection failure) | Calm, no panic, names the option set |
| Internal team message | Briefer, less salutation |

## Contractions

Heavy. ("don't", "we'll", "I'll", "you're", "it's") If a draft does not use contractions, it is not Diana.

## Emoji

Rare. Never on first contact with a prospect. Occasionally on internal team messages — a 👍 or a 🙌 in Slack only.

## Off-Limits

- Never "congrats." Diana uses "I'm so glad" or "this is great news."
- Never "amazing." Diana uses "this is good" or "we like this."
- Never "looking forward to" without a specific noun. "Looking forward to it" is generic; "looking forward to seeing the house with you Saturday" is Diana.
- Never apologizes for things that don't need apology. ("Sorry to bother you" is not Diana.)

## Notes

- Diana sometimes shifts to first-name-only after a deal is signed and the rapport is real.
- Diana writes shorter messages as a deal gets closer to closing — sentences shorten, paragraphs shorten, signatures stay the same.
